# Urban Survival

## Learning Objectives

- Threat recognition, situational awareness, route choice, calm movement, teamwork, leadership, observation, quick judgment, communication, and staying safe in busy man-made environments.

## Activity 1 (Hazard Hunt Walkthrough)

### Data

| Field | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Age Limit | 10+ |
| Group Size | 4-10 players per team |
| Duration | 20-25 minutes |
| Prep Time | 5 minutes |
| Difficulty | Easy to Medium |
| Category | Outdoor, Observation, Movement, Safety awareness |
| Items Needed | Open area, paper hazard cards, pen, cone or marker |
| Murabbi Rating |  |

### Setup

Place 6 to 8 hazard cards around the space or hold them in the murabbi’s hand.

Use these urban hazard examples:

- Broken glass on the ground
- Crowded exit with pushing
- Wet floor near a doorway
- A blocked passage
- A loud argument nearby
- An unknown person asking to split the group
- A parked vehicle blocking visibility
- A dark corner with low visibility

### How to Play

1. Divide shabab into teams.
2. Walk the group through the space slowly.
3. At each stop, the murabbi shows one hazard.
4. Teams must say what the threat is and what safe action to take.
5. Teams earn points for noticing details quickly and answering calmly.

### Sample Answers for Murabbi

- Broken glass on the ground: stop, warn others, avoid stepping there, use another route.
- Crowded exit with pushing: slow down, stay together, do not push, wait for space.
- Wet floor near a doorway: walk around it, warn others, do not run.
- A blocked passage: find another exit, inform the murabbi, do not force the way through.
- A loud argument nearby: move away calmly, stay with the group, avoid joining the conflict.
- An unknown person asking to split the group: stay together, inform the murabbi, do not separate.
- A parked vehicle blocking visibility: stop before the blind spot, look carefully, move with caution.
- A dark corner with low visibility: avoid rushing in, stay with the group, use a safer route.

### Murabbi Notes

Keep this focused on safety, not fear. The lesson should train shabab to notice danger early and choose the safest route.

## Activity 2 (Safe Path Challenge)

### Data

| Field | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Age Limit | 10+ |
| Group Size | 4-8 players per team |
| Duration | 15-20 minutes |
| Prep Time | 5 minutes |
| Difficulty | Easy |
| Category | Indoor or Outdoor, Route planning, Team strategy, Problem-solving |
| Items Needed | Cones, chairs, tape, small paper signs |
| Murabbi Rating |  |

### Setup

Create a short path with one safe route and one unsafe route. Mark the unsafe route with signs like `wet`, `blocked`, `crowded`, or `dark`.

Use these route examples:

- Safe route: start, turn left, pass the chair, stop at the cone, finish
- Unsafe route: start, go straight into the blocked area, then stop
- Safe route: start, move around the wet spot, then go to the exit

### How to Play

1. Teams study the setup for 10 seconds.
2. One runner chooses the safest path.
3. The runner returns and explains why the route was safe.
4. Next teammate repeats the challenge.
5. Points go to the team with the best decisions and explanations.

### Creative Twist

Ask teams to give the safe route a code name such as `clean path`, `quiet lane`, or `safe line`, then announce it before moving.

### Murabbi Notes

Use this to teach calm choice-making in crowded streets, schools, markets, and public spaces. Keep it practical and simple.

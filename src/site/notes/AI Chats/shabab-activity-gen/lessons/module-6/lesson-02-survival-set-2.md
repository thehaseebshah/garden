# Survival Set 2

## Learning Objectives

- Emergency response, basic first aid awareness, calm communication, quick assessment, teamwork, resourcefulness, prioritization, safety discipline, helping others, trust in Allah, and practical readiness.

## Activity 1 (First Response Sort)

### Data

| Field | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Age Limit | 10+ |
| Group Size | 4-8 players per team |
| Duration | 15-20 minutes |
| Prep Time | 5-10 minutes |
| Difficulty | Easy to Medium |
| Category | Indoor, Team strategy, First aid awareness, Problem-solving |
| Items Needed | Scenario cards, paper, pens, stopwatch |
| Murabbi Rating |  |

### Setup

Prepare 8 to 10 scenario cards, or read the situations aloud.

Use these plug-and-play examples:

- Nosebleed during class
- Minor cut from paper or a small object
- Someone twists an ankle while walking
- A teammate feels dizzy after running
- Someone looks pale and weak
- A bottle of water has spilled and the floor is slippery
- A person complains of stomach pain
- A teammate is upset, panicking, or crying
- Someone feels too hot after outdoor activity
- A person drops to the ground and needs immediate adult help

### How to Play

1. Split shabab into teams.
2. Read one scenario card at a time.
3. Teams have 45-60 seconds to say the first safe response.
4. Teams must answer in order: what to do first, who to inform, and what not to do.
5. Murabbi awards points for calmness, correct prioritization, and teamwork.

### Sample Answers for Murabbi

- Nosebleed during class: sit down, lean slightly forward, inform the murabbi, use tissue or cloth, do not tilt the head back.
- Minor cut from paper or a small object: wash hands if possible, inform the murabbi, clean the area, apply a bandage if available.
- Someone twists an ankle while walking: stop activity, help the person sit, inform the murabbi, avoid forcing them to walk.
- A teammate feels dizzy after running: stop immediately, move to shade or a cool area, give water if allowed, call the murabbi.
- Someone looks pale and weak: keep them seated, inform the murabbi at once, do not crowd them, wait for adult guidance.
- A bottle of water has spilled and the floor is slippery: warn others, stop movement in that area, clean it quickly, prevent falls.
- A person complains of stomach pain: inform the murabbi, keep them calm, avoid giving random advice, wait for adult decision.
- A teammate is upset, panicking, or crying: speak calmly, keep the group quiet, bring the murabbi, do not tease or overwhelm them.
- Someone feels too hot after outdoor activity: move to shade, loosen heavy clothing if needed, rest, give water, inform the murabbi.
- A person drops to the ground and needs immediate adult help: call the murabbi immediately, clear space, do not crowd, follow adult instructions.

### Murabbi Notes

Keep this lesson simple and calm. The goal is not advanced medical training; it is recognizing danger early, calling for help quickly, and staying organized.

## Activity 2 (Emergency Supply Pack)

### Data

| Field | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Age Limit | 10+ |
| Group Size | 4-10 players per team |
| Duration | 15-20 minutes |
| Prep Time | 10 minutes |
| Difficulty | Easy |
| Category | Indoor, Sorting game, Resource acquisition, Teamwork |
| Items Needed | Mixed items, table or floor space, paper labels |
| Murabbi Rating |  |

### Setup

Place mixed items in the middle. Include useful items, unnecessary items, and a few distractors.

Use these examples:

- Water bottle
- Dates or snack bar
- Small torch
- Whistle
- Bandage
- Tissue pack
- Notebook and pen
- Scarf or cloth
- Toy car
- Extra shoe
- Empty wrapper
- Plastic spoon

### How to Play

1. Announce a scenario such as injury, heat, rain, or getting separated.
2. Teams choose the 5 most useful items for that scenario.
3. Each team explains why they chose them.
4. Murabbi compares answers and gives points for practicality.

### Scenario Prompts

- A short outdoor trip in hot weather
- A small injury during group movement
- A rainy walk back to the bus
- Waiting in a place with no electricity

### Sample Sorting Guide

- Hot weather: water bottle, snack bar, cloth, torch, whistle
- Small injury: bandage, tissue pack, water bottle, notebook and pen
- Rainy walk: cloth, torch, whistle, water bottle, extra covering if available
- No electricity: torch, whistle, water bottle, snack bar, notebook and pen

### Murabbi Notes

This activity should feel practical, not theoretical. Ask shabab to justify choices in simple words: useful now, useful later, or not needed.

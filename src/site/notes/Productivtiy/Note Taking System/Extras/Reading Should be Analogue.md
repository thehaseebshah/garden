Physical books are tactile, create memory, and tech does not have barakah. Check [[Productivtiy/Note Taking System/Analog vs Digital Note Taking\|Analog vs Digital Note Taking]]. Only reference books should be on eInk. Core books should be physical. Quran should be physical, both the portable and at-home version.

Detailed reasons:
- You see physical books and shelves and see how much you've read
- You're more likely to re-read your notes
- Your family could see them, and get inspired to read, has positive correlation with educated children.
- You could give as gifts, 
- Studies say you absorb more from physical books
	- They are easier on the eyes
	- They are better for sleep hygiene (because you're not on a device at night )
- It's an experience 
- Library is tied to better idea generation and creativity 
- People on screens learn less, 49 separate studies (a meta analysis)
- Reading on paper is like exploring a landscape, building a mental map, skimming, re-reading. Digital readers read linearly. Embodied anchoring uses the spatial patterns like coffee stains on the page. It's a multi-sensory experience.
- Reading on paper activates beta and gamma waves, prefrontal cortex shows less activity. Digital reading activates theta and alpha waves associating with idling. 
- Print readers read faster.
- There's a shallowing effect on screens, digital screens are associated with skimming and looking for keywords. The media encourages superficial processing.
- Confidence trap: digital readers feel more confident but perform low.

Arguments for Digital:
- Your read more
- Cal Newport: Use all formats don't overthink it. All have their own benefits.
- Books are expensive 
- Audio is even faster 
- For anything that's not complex or doesn't require retention digital is fine

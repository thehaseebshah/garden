- Ideas in notes can be implemented, either as habits or general lessons that you can use at some point in life
- Things can be memorized for the sake of memorization
- Things can be memorized for bringing up in outputs impromptu.
- Looking at notes can answer your inner questions, and you can understand things and concepts better and can grow in your knowledge
- Items can be kept for future motivation 
- They can increase your love for someone 
- Tasks and Events can be planned and completed
- Memories and Tracked Items can be kept
	- For future joy
	- For future contemplation
- Items can be kept for future reference
- Reading is fast, but thinking is slow. Writing by hand or typing minimal notes forces your reading speed to match your processing speed, ensuring deep, synchronous focus rather than passive skimming.
- The human brain can only hold a few complex thoughts in working memory simultaneously. Notes serve as an immediate cognitive scratchpad, allowing you to hold, compare, and connect conflicting quotes or complex ideas side-by-side without straining your mental bandwidth.
* Because *you* wrote the note in your own words, rereading it months or years later does not just retrieve data—it instantly reactivates the exact emotional, intellectual, and environmental context you were in when you first formed the thought.
* Periodically reviewing deeply curated notes on core values, life rules, and foundational philosophies acts as an internal compass, systematically fortifying your worldview against the distracting noise of modern digital media.
* Using a "Just-In-Time" note-taking strategy provides an immediate launchpad for creative output. It eliminates the blank-page syndrome by giving you an organized, pre-synthesized armor of arguments ready to be deployed into your next blog post or project.

### **Zone 1: Basic Privacy & Security**
1. **Password Management**:
   - Avoid using browser or OS password managers.
   - Use a password manager like **Bitwarden** (cloud-based) or **KeePass** (local storage).
   - Create strong, unique passwords for each account.
   - Generate random answers for security questions and store them in your password manager.
   - Use long, complex passwords for your devices and memorize them.
   - Secure your router with a strong password and enable WPA2 encryption.
   - Disable biometrics in high-risk areas (e.g., airports, protests).

2. **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**:
   - Avoid SMS-based 2FA; use local authenticator apps like **Tofu** (iOS), **Aegis** (Android), or hardware keys.
   - Back up your 2FA codes separately to avoid getting locked out.

3. **Minimize Digital Footprint**:
   - Disable tracking on **Google My Activity**.
   - Delete unused accounts and replace personal information with fake data using services like **Gorilla Mail**.
   - Use sites like **JustDeleteMe.xyz** to find and delete old accounts.

4. **Social Media Privacy**:
   - Use basic privacy settings.
   - Avoid friending strangers, commenting on public posts, or sharing personal information.
   - Do not grant unnecessary permissions to apps (e.g., location tracking).

5. **VPN Usage**:
   - Use a trusted VPN to encrypt traffic and mask your IP address.
   - Recommended VPNs: **ProtonVPN**, **Mullvad**, **IVPN**, **Windscribe**, and **Surfshark**.

6. **Secure Communication**:
   - Avoid SMS; use encrypted messaging apps like **Signal**.
   - Switch to privacy-focused email providers like **ProtonMail** or **Tutanota**.
   - Use disposable email services like **33Mail**, **Gorilla Mail**, or **TempMail**.

7. **Browser Security**:
   - Use trusted extensions and privacy-respecting search engines like **DuckDuckGo** or **Startpage**.
   - Clear cookies and browsing data frequently.
   - Use incognito mode when necessary.

8. **Smartphone Privacy**:
   - Disable analytics, telemetry, and unnecessary app permissions.
   - Turn off Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, NFC, and GPS when not in use.
   - Enable MAC address randomization (iOS 14+ and Android 10+).
   - Keep your device and apps updated.

9. **File Management**:
   - Clear temporary files and logs regularly using tools like **BleachBit**.
   - Securely delete sensitive files (do not rely on the recycle bin).
   - Back up your data (on-site and off-site).

10. **Real-World Privacy**:
    - Avoid using public computers for personal information.
    - Be cautious with smartwatches, fitness trackers, and IoT devices.
    - Use safes and shredders for physical documents.

### **Zone 2: Enhanced Privacy & Security**
1. **Disable Biometrics Completely**:
   - Use strong passwords instead of biometrics for better security.

2. **Delete Google Account**:
   - **Remove your Google account to reduce data collection.**
   - **Use company provided Google Account if you have one or an Al Burhan Google account.**
   - **For storage use the company account and Yandex.**
   - **For subscriptions use the Yandex email**
   - **For private emails use the Proton Mail account.**

3. **Pseudonyms & Separate Accounts**:
   - Create pseudonyms for specific tasks (e.g., shopping, social media).
   - Use **VoIP services** (e.g., Google Voice, Burner) for phone numbers.

4. **Social Media Anonymity**:
   - Avoid posting personal photos or identifiable information.
   - Use progressive web apps instead of native social media apps.

5. **Browser Separation**:
   - Use different browsers for different purposes (e.g., **Tor** for anonymity, **Firefox** for personal use).
   - Harden Firefox for privacy and security.

6. **Free & Open Source Software (FOSS)**:
   - Switch to FOSS alternatives for your daily apps.
   - Use **F-Droid** for Android or **AltStore** for iOS to find FOSS apps.
   - Consider using **Linux** for your desktop OS.
   - Switch Apps on [[Tech/Digital Tools I am Using and Why\|Digital Tools I am Using and Why]]

7. **Device Security**:
   - Use **iOS** or **Type 2 Android** devices for better privacy.
   - Consider custom ROMs like **GrapheneOS** or **CalyxOS** for maximum privacy.

8. **Computer Security**:
   - Password-protect your BIOS and encrypt your drives.
   - Use **VeraCrypt** for file-level encryption.
   - Spoof your MAC address for additional anonymity.

9. **Metadata Removal**:
   - Strip metadata from files using tools like **mat2** or EXIF removers.

10. **Home Security**:
    - Install security cameras and alarms.
    - Use cable locks for laptops and intrusion kits for desktops.
    - Remove IoT devices from your home.

### **Zone 3: Extreme Privacy & Security**
1. **Delete Social Media & Online Accounts**:
   - Export and delete your data from social media platforms.
   - Falsify information before deleting accounts.
   - Delete [[Tech/My Online Accounts to Delete\|My Online Accounts to Delete]]

2. **Advanced Browser Techniques**:
   - Use **hardened Firefox** for personal accounts.
   - Use **Tor** for anonymity and casual browsing.
   - Be aware of behavioral tracking and social engineering attacks.

3. **Ditch Smartphones**:
   - Use **dumb phones** or custom ROMs like **GrapheneOS** or **CalyxOS**.
   - Consider not having a phone at all and relying on VoIP.

4. **Operating Systems**:
   - Use **Linux** for privacy and security.
   - Consider specialized OS like **Tails OS** (anonymity) or **Qubes OS** (security).

5. **Anonymous Purchases**:
   - Use cash for in-person purchases.
   - For online purchases, use gift cards, non-reloadable Visa cards, or privacy-focused cryptocurrencies like **Monero**.

6. **Physical Privacy**:
   - Remove or disable cameras and microphones on devices.
   - Use external webcams and microphones.
   - Wear sunglasses, masks, or hats to avoid facial recognition.

7. **Advocacy**:
   - Educate friends and family about privacy.
   - Normalize privacy by advocating for it in your community.

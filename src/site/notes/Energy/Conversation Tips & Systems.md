## Post-Interaction Ritual
*Immediately after a call, spend 30 seconds updating their `[[Person Note]]`.*
* **Log:** New job details, names of family members, specific struggles, upcoming dates (surgery, trips).
* **Next Time:** Review this note *before* calling to ask specific follow-up questions.

## The "Storyworthy" Bank
*My life is the content. Stop saying "Not much" when asked what's new.*

**Capture Strategy:**
When something funny, frustrating, or weird happens, log it in `[[Daily Note]]`

**Structure of a Good Micro-Story:**
1.  **The Hook:** "I made a huge mistake..." (Not: "I went shopping.")
2.  **The Struggle:** "I couldn't find the limes and argued with a machine."
3.  **The Resolution:** "Turns out, I was scanning a lemon."

## Conversation Starters (The Opener)

### The "Update" Replacements
* "What is the **best thing** that happened to you this week?"
* "What is the **most annoying** thing you had to deal with today?" (Venting creates bonds).
* "Working on any personal projects lately, or just trying to survive?"

### For Family (Nostalgia)
* "I was just thinking about [Relative/Pet]. Do you have a favorite memory of them?"
* "What is one meal you wish you could eat right now?"
* "Who do you think I take after more, Mom or Dad?"

### For Friends (Fun/Hypothetical)
* "If you could teleport anywhere for dinner right now, where would we go?"
* "Watched anything lately that was actually good, or is it all trash?"
* "Unpopular opinion time: I think [Popular Thing] is overrated. Do you agree?"

### Deep / Connection
* "What’s a habit you’re trying to break right now?"
* "What is the biggest thing giving you stress this week?"
* "Do you feel like you have more or less free time compared to last year?"

## Keeping the Conversation Going (The Middle)

### On Calls / In Person
**The FORD Method** (Topics that always work):
* **F**amily / Friends
* **O**ccupation / School
* **R**ecreation (Books, Movies, Hobbies)
* **D**reams / Plans (Travel, Holidays)

**Conversation Threading:**
Listen for "hooks" (nouns/verbs) to ask follow-up questions.
* *Statement:* "I was tired so I just cooked dinner."
* *Thread 1 (Emotion):* "Why so tired? Busy week?"
* *Thread 2 (Activity):* "I love cooking. What did you make?"

**General to Specific:**
* *Instead of:* "How is work?"
* *Ask:* "Is your boss still driving you crazy with [Specific Issue]?"

### On WhatsApp
**Show, Don't Tell:**
Send a photo of your environment/food instead of asking "What's up?"

**The Double-Text Opener:**
Never send just "Hi".
* "Hey! 👋 I was just listening to that song you recommended. It's stuck in my head."

**Voice Notes:**
Use for stories that are too long to type. Bridge the gap between text and call.

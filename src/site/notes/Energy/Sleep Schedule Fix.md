### Part 1: How to Sleep Right Now
* **Brain Dump:** Write out any racing thoughts or tomorrow's to-do list on physical paper to clear your mind.
* **20-Minute Reset:** If awake after 20 minutes, get out of bed. Go to a dimly lit room and only return when your eyelids feel heavy after a low stimulus activity, engaging your mind without screens or excitement:
	* Read a dull, dense, or dry physical book.
	* Listen to a dedicated sleep podcast or a dry topic with zero personal stakes.
	* Listen to a familiar podcast or story where you already know what happens next.
- Then get back to bed:
	* **"SILENT" Trick (Cognitive Shuffling):** Think of a word like "SILENT." For each letter, visualize objects starting with that letter (e.g., S = Sheep, Shadow, Soup) to scramble analytical thinking.
	* **Breathing Technique:** Do the Physiological Sigh—take two sharp inhales through the nose, followed by one long, slow exhale through the mouth. Repeat 3 to 5 times.

### Part 2: How to Fix the Routine
* **Anchor Wake Time:** Set a strict morning wake-up alarm and stick to it, no matter how little sleep you got the night before.
* **Light as a Switch:**
* *Morning:* Get 10–15 minutes of bright sunlight in your eyes immediately upon waking to stop melatonin.
* *Evening:* Dim all overhead lights and eliminate blue screen light after sunset to signal bedtime.

* **Food Anchor:** Delay your first meal until your new, desired morning wake time to biologically signal that the day has started.
* **Delay, Don't Force (Optional):** If your schedule is severely flipped, push your bedtime *forward* by 1 to 2 hours each day to circle back to normal, rather than forcing it backward.
